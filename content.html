Beauty & The Beast — Minimalist Movie Poster

A minimalist reimagining of the Disney classic using shape, silhouette, and emotional tone. The design features Belle and Beast’s dance in soft blue hues, accompanied by the tagline: “Who could ever learn to love a beast.” This project honed composition, symbolism, and balance through typography and form.

Tools: Adobe Photoshop Focus: Typography · Composition · Color Theory
Photoshop · Vintage Design

Tokyo, Japan — Retro Travel Postcard

Designed a retro postcard combining found imagery, typography, and pattern work. This collage captures Tokyo’s skyline through a vintage lens, layered with kanji and English text for cultural duality. Aged textures and selective desaturation evoke nostalgia and wanderlust.

Tools: Adobe Photoshop Focus: Collage · Texture · Typography Hierarchy
Digital Art · Conceptual Design

“Strings Attached” — Trompe L’oeil Self Portrait

A conceptual composite where my back forms the body of a guitar — exploring connection between art, self, and sound. This exercise refined my non-destructive editing, blending, and realism techniques while integrating body form with inanimate texture for an uncanny illusion.

Tools: Adobe Photoshop Focus: Photo Manipulation · Lighting · Layering
